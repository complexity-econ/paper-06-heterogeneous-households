\begin{abstract}
We extend the SFC-ABM model of UBI-driven automation (Papers~1--5) with
heterogeneous household agents---each with individual savings, debt, rent
obligations, skills, and health scarring from unemployment. Where previous
work used an aggregate household sector that computed welfare metrics
post-hoc, individual agents reveal within-group inequality, path-dependent
poverty traps, and bankruptcy cascades invisible to the representative agent.
We characterize how UBI interacts with household heterogeneity across five
simulation campaigns: backward-compatibility validation, full BDP sweep,
MPC heterogeneity, wealth distribution sensitivity, and retraining policy
analysis. Results show that [TBD].
\end{abstract}
